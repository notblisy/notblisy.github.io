Lore

The Ursine are ancient beings, some of the oldest in the world, predating most races of man. It's unclear as to how they came about, as they do not have a written history or language of their own, and all known about Ursine past is through stories from one Ursine to another, or written documentation from other peoples.

Appearance

Ursine range from 5 to 10ft. tall depending on subrace, and can be a variety of colors, from white to brown to black. Usually they are on the larger end, and are always bulky with both muscle and fat. There is no visual difference between males and females, including their voice, which is often a deep grumble.

Ursine Names

An Ursine has two names, a given name, and a familial name. They are then combined, and pronounced as one name, with the familial name being capitalized to indicate the split. The one who raises the child is which family name that child is given. Names are simple, often grunt sounding.

Example Names: TerraBor, ShinTo, FeroSil, KorFar.

Love no one but the Earth

Ursine tend to be very solitary and cold towards most people, even other Ursine. While they are cordial and do not mean disrespect, it is just because they are solitary. They usually only meet if it is important, and even if they have Ursine spouses it is not uncommon for them to go years without seeing eachother. The one thing they are warm to, is the Earth. Ursine have a deep connection with Nature and value the land they live in above all else, and would do anything to protect it from harm.

Here's a Claw for you. Your claws are natural weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with them, you deal slashing damage equal to 1d4 + your Strength modifier, instead of the normal damage.
Language. Your character can speak, read, and write Common and Ursine.
Subrace. You choose from one of seven subraces of Ursine:

Polar Ursine

Born and raised in the artic of this world, the Polar Ursine have to fight for every scrap of food and shelter they can because of the limited resources in the area. As a result, they have very harsh personalities and have a lot of difficulty trusting other with their resources.

Ability Score Increase. Your Constitution score increases by 1.
The Tundra's Claws. Once per day you may empower your unarmed Melee attacks to deal an additional 1d4 cold damage for 3 rounds, and enemies hit must make a constitution saving throw. If they fail, their movement is reduced by 10ft. Each enemy may only be slowed once. The damage increases by 1d4 at levels 6, 11, and 16. You regain this feature after a long rest.
The cold never bothered me anyway. Because of a life lived in the arctic, the Polar Ursine are immune to nonmagical cold damage, and resist magical cold damage.

Grizzly Ursine

Living in the forests, plains, and occasionally cities, the Grizzly Ursine is the most social Ursine with the races of man, and is the one most people will have seen. They are quick to agitate, often live alone, and are the most ferocios of the Ursine when angered.

Ability Score Increase. Your Strength Score increases by 1.
Grizzlies Ferocity. The Grizzly ursine may once a day become ferociously angry, empowering its unarmed strikes this round to deal an extra 1d4 damage, and the target must make a con saving throw. If failed they are stunned. damage increases by 1d4 at 6, 11, 16. You regain this feature after a long rest.

Forest Ursine

The Forest Ursine are extrmelely mobile and elusive. They tend towards a smaller size, and usually avoid conflict at all cost. Probably the least confrontational of the Ursine, the Forest Ursine wishes to hide in its forest and live amongst it, and nothing else.

Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity score increases by 1.
Adjusted Size. The Forest Ursines max Height is 5ft. 5 Inches, and their Max weight is 200lbs.
Arboreal Movement. You have 20ft. of Climb speed.
Mask of Nature. You can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, and other natural phenomena.
Forests Will. You may choose one 1st level spell from the Druid Spell list and use it once per day after a long rest. You may choose another spell at 6, 11, and 16. You may choose the same spell twice.

Sun Ursine

Spending their days atop mountains, the Sun Ursine derive most of their power from the Sun, and have a unique connection to it. Most of their days are spent lounging about relaxing in it.

Ability Score Increase. Your Intelligence Score increases by 1.
Solar Flare. Choose a 15 ft area once a day to cause an extremely bright flash of sunlight in, enemies must make con saving throw or be blinded for 1 round. Range increases by 5ft at 6, 11, 16. You regain this feature after a long rest.
Flameo, hotsman. The Sun Ursine is immune to nonmagical fire damage, and resists magical fire damage.

Panda Ursine

The Panda Ursine spend their days seeking to be at peace with themselves and their inner mind. They seek to find a purpose in themselves and their place in the world. Whatever path in life they choose, they will probably be the most devoted person you know towards that subject, even if they are not the best. It is not uncommon

Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity Score increases by 1.
Monks Training. The panda ursine once a day can use one of the following skills: flurry of blows, step of the wind, and patient defense all as a bonus action. gain 1 extra use at 6, 11, 16. You gain all charges back on a short or long rest.
Intense Meditation. Once per day, you may use an additional hit die during a short rest witout expending it.

Cave Ursine

The most Ancient Ursine, these behemoths command respect to all creatures who are near them. They spend their entire lives in dens they pick out when they are young, and while they would defend the location ferociously, they often do not have to, as most things know to not mess with a Cave Ursine, even other powerful Ursine.

Ability Score Increase. Your Constitution Score increases by 1.
Lumbering Giant. Once Per day the Cave Ursine may, at the start of initiative, choose to cut its movement speed to 20, but be uneffected by difficult terrain, immune to prone, and immune to grapple conditions for one minute. Once the minute ends, your move speed returns to normal. The number of times you can do this increases at 6 and 16 to two and three respectivelly. You regain this feature after long rest.
Giant. The Cave Ursing counts as 1 size larger for carrying weight, mounting, and grapple.

Sloth Ursine

The Sloth Ursine are a rare breed, known for sleeping the day away and lazily using their Vaccumed Bite to suck fruit off of trees, these non-confrontational creatures tend to roam around the land, finding new spots each day to relax in.
