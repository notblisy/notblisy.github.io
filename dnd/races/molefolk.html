Mole-Folk

Introduction

We do not tolerate lies in this city, exclaimed Si-Hal-Ette. The king of the great city-state of Moleantis was not pleased. Before him sat two theives, begging for forgiveness, claiming their ways would change. The two had been harassing local shipments going from the city-state to its territories for weeks, and it became apparent through the Zone of Truth that their intentions were not going to change. "I may be forgiving, but I am not foolish or weak. I gave you a chance for truth, but you squandered it. Throw them away while they await their trial."
A spartanly people, most Mole-Folk value Truth, Strength, and their community above all else. While Moles often spend their days within their kingdom working to improve upon their craft, lives, and the lives of those around them, many recognize the only way to spread the prosperity and quality of life they have is to leave.

Punished by a God

Once the capital city of a large human majority empire that dominated the lands because of its location near magic ore mines, the city-state of Moleantis was forced underground and sealed magically from the outside worled, with its population turned into large humanoid moles by a divine power to restore balance to the world and punish the empire for its greed.

This caused a split in the residents, and those that believed the only way forward was to accept this punishment by worshiping the gods, rejecting the magics that caused such greed, and learning to live morally stayed in the city. There, however, were those that felt resentful of such a punishment and rejected such ideas. These people left the bounds of the city, looting and scrounging to survive.

Appearance

Moles range from six to eight feet tall, with short fur that can be varying shades of brown to black. This color fades to grey or salt and pepper as they grow old. Male moles also tend to grow thin facial hair at the end of their snout.

Mole-Folk Names

Moles have three names, one given, one picked, and one family. Each name is only one syllable, and is divided by a dash. The first name is given by the parents, the middle is chosen by themselves once adulthood is reached, and the last is the family name. In formal situations, the full name is referenced, and the full name is always used when speaking to a Mole in a position of power over you, such as an elder relative, a superior at work, or the King. In casual situations just the first name is typically used, though some Moles to prefer their chosen name. Names are not gendered in any way.

Example Names: Va-Rha-Shall, Si-Hal-Ette, Ti-So-Rhan, Ki-Kor-Elle, San-Vi-Tahr, Kor-Ja-Ku, Ran-Jon-Si, Sho-Fu-Lore, Mo-U-To, Gah-En-Vi, Il-Er-Shu, Mur-Dra-Fell.

Friends to all, but their enemies.

Moles are the type of folk who trust first, but are insanely dangerous to cross. Because they once were humans, and share a similar lifespan and perspective, they get along with almost all races easily. Except any of the dark under races such as Drow, Duergar, and Svirfneblin, because these are the ones that are most likely to cause trouble in their city which often resides near the under.

Mole-Folk Traits

Your Mole-Folk has certain characteristics in common with all other Moles.

Ability Score Increase. Your Strength Score increases by 2.
Age. Moles age at the same rate as humans, reaching adulthood at 18, and live to be around 150 years old.
Alignment. Mole-Folk very rarely end up as evil, most often they are defined by their upbringing. Citizens are typically lawful and good, while Apostates tend to be neutral, and can land anywhere on that spectrum.
Size. Moles are between 6-8 ft. tall, and weight an average of 250-300 pounds.
Speed. Your base walking speed is 35 ft. You also have 10 ft. of burrow.
Darkvision. Accustomed to life underground, you have superior dark vision in all dark and dim conditions. You can see dim light within 60 ft. of you as if it were brightly lit, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You cannot discern the color of darkness, only shades of gray.
Mole Claws. Your claws are natural weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with them, you deal slashing damage equal to 1d4 + your Strength modifier, instead of the normal damage.
Language. Your character can speak, read, and write Common and Undercommon.
Subrace. Two subraces of Mole exist, based on the dynamic of the city-state. Choose from one of these subraces:

Citizen

Raised in Moleantis, or a territory of the city-state, a Citizen has served their time in the military, and shares many of the values of Truth, Strength, and practicality. Naturally strong and charismatic, these Moles believe in their society, their God, and wish the world to be as good Moleantis.

Ability Score Increase. Your Charisma score increases by 1.
Truth Above All. You may cast the spell "Zone of Truth" once per day with this skill. Charisma is the saving throw.
Anti-Magic Training. Training provided by their military service grants Citizens the ability to easily see openings while Wizards cast spells complacently. When a creature within reach casts a spell, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against that creature.
Moleantian Military Training. Gain Proficiency in War picks, medium armor, and the religion skill.

Apostate

Living outside the city, resenting the gods for their curse, these moles rely on stealth, theivery, and their cunning to survive. Often scraping by, living a meager existence, they take whatever they can get without remorse.

Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity Score increases by 1.
Take What You Can. You gain the Mage hand cantrip, along with mage hand ledgerman, which lets you use theives tools, pick pockets, and turn mage hand invisible. You can do this twice per day. Also you gain proficiency in theives tools.
Apostate Weapon Training. You gain Proficiency in Daggers, shortswords, and hand crossbows.
A Quick Escape. You can use swift burrow once per day, letting you not take an attack of opportunity while using burrow movement.
